Concrete vs. Abstract Nouns

Test your grammar knowledge with 20 questions

How to Play

Determine whether each noun is concrete or abstract.

  • Concrete nouns refer to physical things that can be perceived with the senses (touch, sight, hearing, smell, taste).
  • Abstract nouns refer to ideas, qualities, or states that cannot be perceived with the senses.

Example: "apple" is concrete (you can see and taste it), while "love" is abstract (it's an emotion).
