Pronoun & Time/Place Changes

Transform direct speech to reported speech by adjusting pronouns, possessive adjectives, time expressions, and place words.

Key Changes:

Pronouns: I → he/she · we → they · my → his/her
Time: now → then · today → that day · yesterday → the day before
Place: here → there · this → that · these → those
Example: "I am happy," said Tom → Tom said that he was happy.
Tip: Always consider the reporter's perspective. First/second person pronouns shift to third person unless reporting about yourself.

Time & Tense Shifts

Master tense backshifting and time expression changes when reporting speech from a past perspective.

Tense Backshifting:

Present Simple → Past Simple: "I am busy" → he was busy
Present Continuous → Past Continuous: "I am reading" → she was reading
will → would · can → could · may → might
Example: "I am busy," said Tom → Tom said that he was busy.
Exception: General truths or still-true facts can keep present tense: "The sun rises in the east" → He said the sun rises in the east.

Reported Questions & Commands

Learn how to report yes/no questions, WH-questions, and imperative commands correctly.

Question Patterns:

Yes/No: "Do you live here?" → He asked if I lived there.
WH-: "Where is the station?" → She asked where the station was.
Commands: "Close the door" → He told me to close the door.
Example: "Do you live here?" → He asked _______ I lived there. (if/whether)
Remember: Reported questions use statement word order (no inversion), no question mark, and add "if/whether" for yes/no questions.
