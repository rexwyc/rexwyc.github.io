Adverbs of Manner

Describe how an action is performed. They usually come after the main verb or after the object.

Examples:

Quickly: She ran quickly.
Carefully: He drove carefully.
Loudly: They spoke loudly.

Adverbs of Place

Indicate where an action happens. They often come after the main verb or the object.

Examples:

Here/There: Please sit here.
Everywhere: I looked everywhere.
Inside/Outside: Let's go outside.

They include words like abroad, upstairs, downtown, and nearby.

Adverbs of Time

Tell us when something happens. They can be placed at the beginning or end of a sentence.

Examples:

Now/Then: We'll eat now.
Today/Tomorrow: Tomorrow is Monday.
Already/Yet: I've already seen it.

Includes definite time (yesterday) and indefinite time (recently, soon).

Adverbs of Frequency

Show how often an action occurs. They usually come before the main verb (but after the verb 'to be').

Examples:

Always: She always smiles.
Sometimes: I sometimes walk.
Never: He never forgets.

Other examples: usually, frequently, rarely, seldom, occasionally.

Adverbs of Degree

Indicate the intensity, extent, or level of an action, adjective, or another adverb.

Examples:

Very: It's very hot.
Quite: I'm quite sure.
Almost: We're almost there.

They often modify adjectives or other adverbs: too, enough, extremely, nearly.

Adverb Placement Rules

Where you place an adverb changes the emphasis and meaning. Three main positions: front, mid, end.

Positions:

Front: Suddenly, it rained.
Mid (before verb): He often calls.
End (after verb/obj): She drives carefully.

Frequency adverbs usually go in mid-position. Manner adverbs usually go in end-position.
