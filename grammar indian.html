Common vs. Proper Nouns

Nouns are words that name people, places, things, or ideas. They are divided into two main categories: Common Nouns and Proper Nouns. Understanding the difference between these two types of nouns is fundamental to proper English grammar.

Common Nouns

Common nouns are general names for people, places, things, or ideas. They are not capitalized unless they begin a sentence.

  • Refer to general items, not specific ones
  • Usually not capitalized
  • Examples: city, country, river, book

Proper Nouns

Proper nouns are specific names for particular people, places, things, or ideas. They are always capitalized.

  • Refer to specific, unique items
  • Always capitalized
  • Examples: Mumbai, India, Ganges River, Harry Potter

Examples Comparison

Common Noun Proper Noun Explanation
country India "Country" is a general term, while "India" is a specific country name
river Ganges "River" is a common noun, "Ganges" is the name of a specific river
city Mumbai "City" is general, "Mumbai" is a specific city in India
monument Taj Mahal "Monument" is common, "Taj Mahal" is a specific monument
language Hindi "Language" is common, "Hindi" is a specific language

Important Rule

Proper nouns are always capitalized. This includes the names of people, places, organizations, and specific things. Common nouns are only capitalized when they appear at the beginning of a sentence.
