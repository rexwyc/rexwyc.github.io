Countable vs. Uncountable Nouns

Test your grammar knowledge with 20 challenging questions

How to Play

Determine whether each noun is countable or uncountable.

Countable Nouns:
Can be counted (one apple, two apples, three apples). Have singular and plural forms.
Uncountable Nouns:
Cannot be counted (*one rice, *two rices). Usually have only one form.

Countable nouns refer to things that can be counted as separate items (book, chair, idea).

Uncountable nouns refer to substances, concepts, or masses that can't be divided into separate elements (water, information, rice).
