General names

Not capitalized*

Proper Noun

Specific names

Always capitalized

Quick Tips

Common nouns: Can use "a/an/the" before them (a city, the teacher)
Proper nouns: Specific names of people, places, organizations
Test: If you can put "the" before it and it still makes sense, it's probably common.

Capitalization Rule

Proper nouns ALWAYS start with a capital letter!
Common nouns only capitalize at the start of a sentence.
Examples: dog (common) vs. Rover (proper), river (common) vs. Nile River (proper)
